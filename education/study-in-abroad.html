FAQS

  • Q1. Can one work (full-time/part-time) while pursuing a course in universities abroad?

      Yes! There are a plethora of part-time work opportunities for students enrolled in the universities in that country. In fact, many work part-time there to sponsor their daily costs.

  • Q2. Is there any chance of securing admission to study abroad without giving any proficiency exams?

      No! In order to secure admission in any university, whether its the USA, the UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, or Germany, the IELTS Exam is compulsory. Certain universities also demand a minimum TOEFL score to allow admission.

  • Q3. How to look for job prospects abroad after completion of the course?

      Actually, one doesnt need to wait for the completion of the course to look for jobs. Most universities abroad have departments like the Placement Cell, Student Support, Department for Foreign Students, etc. that shortlist the job prospects and even prepare the students for interviews.

  • Q4. Is there any financial support for students who seek to study abroad?

      Yes! Students can avail low-interest educational loans from the government. If eligible, students can also enroll via a scholarship that actually helps sponsor a huge part of the program. Moreover, students can also work part-time to sponsor living and other costs in no time.

  • Q5. Can students take a drop before enrolling into universities abroad?

      All students are free to take as many years of drop as they want. However, all that time has to be justified in writing to the satisfaction of the university.

  • Q6. Is there any maximum age limit to apply to universities abroad?

      Yes, there is, but not a uniform one. However, if we take the average of all the countries, the maximum age to apply for a course is approximately 23 years.

  • Q7. Are LORs from school teachers and principals sufficient for admission?

      Though theyre necessary, LORs dont guarantee admission even when assisted by the requisite scores. An SOP needs to be there to ensure admission.
